About

The Kashi Vishwanath Temple, situated on the western bank of the holy river Ganges, is one of the most sacred pilgrimage sites in Hinduism. Dedicated to Lord Shiva, the presiding deity of Varanasi, it is home to one of the twelve revered Jyotirlingas. The temple has a long and storied history, having been rebuilt several times, with the current structure commissioned by Queen Ahilyabai Holkar in 1780. Its most striking feature is the massive spire plated in 800 kilograms of pure gold, a contribution made by Maharaja Ranjit Singh. The temple is the soul of Varanasi, reflecting the city's identity as the "City of Light" and a center of spiritual liberation (Moksha). The narrow, vibrant lanes leading to the temple, known as Vishwanath Gali, are filled with the scent of incense and the sound of temple bells. Inside, the atmosphere is electric with devotion, especially during the daily Aarti ceremonies and the festival of Mahashivratri. The recent development of the Kashi Vishwanath Corridor has transformed the complex, providing a direct, grand walkway from the ghats of the Ganges to the temple. To devotees, a visit here is believed to cleanse the soul of all sins and lead one toward eternal peace. It stands not only as a feat of architecture but as the beating heart of Indian spirituality and ancient tradition.
