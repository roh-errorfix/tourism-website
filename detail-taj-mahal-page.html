About

The Taj Mahal, located on the southern bank of the Yamuna River in Agra, India, is widely considered one of the most beautiful structural compositions in the world. Built between 1632 and 1653 by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal, it serves as a grand mausoleum and a symbol of eternal love. The monument is crafted entirely from shimmering white marble, which appears to change colors depending on the sunlight or moonlight, giving it a magical aura. It is a masterpiece of Mughal architecture, blending Persian, Islamic, and Indian styles with perfect symmetry and intricate floral carvings. Recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the New Seven Wonders of the World, the Taj Mahal attracts millions of visitors annually who come to admire its breathtaking reflection pools and iconic central dome.
