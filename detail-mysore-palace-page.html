About

The Mysore Palace, an architectural jewel in Southern India, serves as the official residence of the Wadiyar dynasty and the seat of the Kingdom of Mysore. Designed by British architect Henry Irwin, it is a premier example of the Indo-Saracenic style, blending Hindu, Mughal, Rajput, and Gothic elements. The palace's exterior is defined by majestic stone arches and a five-story tower topped with a gilded dome. Inside, visitors are greeted by the opulent Durbar Hall, featuring intricately carved rosewood doors, silver gates, and vivid stained-glass ceilings. Its floors are adorned with exquisite mosaic tiles, while the walls display rich oil paintings depicting royal processions. The palace is particularly famous for its celebration of the Dasara festival, during which the entire structure is draped in tradition and history. Every Sunday and during public holidays, the palace is illuminated by nearly 100,000 light bulbs, creating a spectacular golden glow against the night sky. It houses a vast collection of royal memorabilia, including the famous golden throne used during ceremonial occasions. Surrounding the main building are sprawling gardens and twelve historic temples, adding to its spiritual and aesthetic grandeur. Today, it stands as a magnificent tribute to India's royal heritage and attracts millions of tourists annually.
